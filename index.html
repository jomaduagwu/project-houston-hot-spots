        About

        Houston is the fourth largest city in the United States and the most populous city in Texas, famous for its large international community and cultural diversity. There are lots of activities to do, ranging from a collection of top-rated museums and performing arts organizations, to parks and green spaces, shopping, sporting events, and a remarkable culinary culture. Depending on the time of the year, you may be able to enjoy the Livestock Show and Rodeo, or participate in the most anticipated foodie event of the year - Houston Restaurant Weeks. Below, you will find some of the top rated attractions in Houston. With so much to do, it can be overwhelming to find something to do. Houston Hot Spots is the place to go to find current events, shopping, dining, parks, hotels, attractions, and other things to do in the city.         Top Attractions

        Some of the top rated attractions in Houston include - The Space Center Houston, a must-see attraction in Houston and the official visitor center of NASA’s Johnson Space Center. It offers extensive science education programs and a space museum that is fun for all ages. The Museum District is home to 19 museums, of which 10 are free to the public. Most are within easy walking distance of each other, and cover everything from nature, art, history, science and more. Some of the highlights include - Museum of Fine Arts, Houston Museum of Natural Science, Children’s Museum of Houston, and Menil Collection. The Galleria Mall is the largest mall in Texas. There is an abundant array of shopping choices, fine dining options, hotels, and an ice skating rink. Houston Zoo is home to over 6000 exotic and indigenous animals from all over the globe and is the second most visited zoo in the United States. It is located within Hermann Park, another top attraction to checkout. Hermann Park is a 445 acre urban park with lots of things to do and see including a Japanese Garden, Friendship Pavilion, a golf course, miniature train, Miller Outdoor Theatre, McGovern Centennial Garden, sculpture walk, lakes, fountains, pedal boats and trails. The Gerald D. Hines Waterwall Park is a 64-ft high semi-circular water wall and one of the most visited attractions in Houston. It is great for a photo op. The Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo is the world’s largest rodeo and livestock exhibition. It is an annual event that typically happens around February and March. There are nightly concerts, an outdoor carnival, horse shows, barbecues, and so much more. You can also take a day trip to Galveston and enjoy the beautiful beaches, seaside dining, and visit Moody Gardens or the Pleasure Pier.
